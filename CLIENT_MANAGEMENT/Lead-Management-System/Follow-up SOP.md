**Department:** Client Management

**Owner:** Sales Department

**AI Agent:** Sales Manager AI

**Version:** 1.0

---

# Purpose

This SOP defines the standard process for following up with prospective clients after a proposal or quotation has been shared.

The objective is to maximize conversion while maintaining a professional and respectful client experience.

---

# Scope

Applies to:

- Residential Interior Design Projects
- Single Room Projects
- Complete Home Projects
- Online Consultation Services

---

# Principles

Follow-ups must:

- Be professional
- Add value
- Never pressure the client
- Answer questions promptly
- Build trust
- Respect the client's decision

---

# Follow-up Timeline

## Day 0

Proposal Sent

Status

Proposal Sent

Action

Thank the client.

Confirm proposal delivery.

Ask if any clarification is required.

---

## Day 2

Objective

Confirm proposal review.

Message

"Hi [Client Name],

Hope you're doing well.

Just checking whether you had the opportunity to review the proposal.

Please let us know if you have any questions.

We're happy to explain every detail."

Update CRM.

---

## Day 5

Objective

Resolve objections.

Topics

Budget

Timeline

Deliverables

Payment Terms

Design Process

If client has concerns:

Arrange a discussion.

---

## Day 7

Objective

Decision Follow-up

Message

"Hi [Client Name],

We wanted to check whether you've had time to discuss the proposal with your family.

If you'd like to make any adjustments to the scope, we'd be happy to help."

---

## Day 10

Final Professional Follow-up

Message

"Hi [Client Name],

We completely understand if you're still evaluating your options.

Whenever you're ready, we'd be delighted to assist with your project.

Thank you for considering VAYRA Atelier."

---

# Follow-up Rules

Maximum Follow-ups

4

Minimum Gap

2 Days

Never send multiple reminders on the same day.

Always personalize communication.

Never pressure the client.

Never repeatedly ask

"Have you decided?"

Instead ask

"Is there anything we can clarify or improve?"

---

# Response Handling

If Client Says

"I need time."

Status

Negotiation

Next Follow-up

After 5 days

---

If Client Says

"Budget is high."

Actions

Review scope.

Offer phased design if appropriate.

Do not reduce quality.

Discounts require CEO approval.

---

If Client Says

"I chose another designer."

Actions

Thank the client.

Request feedback.

Close lead respectfully.

Maintain relationship.

---

If Client Says

"Call me later."

Update follow-up date.

Schedule reminder.

---

# Lost Lead Policy

A lead becomes Lost when:

No response after final follow-up.

Client cancels.

Budget mismatch.

Project postponed indefinitely.

Client selects another designer.

---

# Lead Re-engagement

After 60 Days

Send:

Portfolio Updates

Completed Projects

Festival Greetings

New Service Announcements

Never send promotional spam.

---

# Communication Standards

Always:

Use client's name.

Maintain polite language.

Respond within business hours.

Keep messages concise.

Answer all questions honestly.

Avoid sales pressure.

Maintain premium brand tone.

# Success Criteria

A follow-up process is successful when:

✓ Every proposal receives scheduled follow-ups.

✓ Client questions are answered promptly.

✓ Communication remains professional.

✓ Lead status is updated accurately.

✓ Sales pipeline reflects the latest information.

✓ Won or Lost outcome is recorded.
